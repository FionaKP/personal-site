Don't you just love it when a project that you've been able to see cleary in your head finally starts becoming a reality? Because that's exactly what I'm feeling right now and its pretty awesome.

For a little context: I have been messing with the hero page of this site for longer than I care to admit. A HUGE reason for that is beccause of the way I had the backgroun image. I followed some random tutorial I found because I wanted the photo to fit a certain way and in order to do so I used something like so:

<img src="hero.jpg" alt="My Background Image">

I obviously did plenty of styling on top of it that I would like to pretend never happened but I'll give you the rundown only if you insist. Basicalyyyyy, I made absolutely everything on the entire page NEED to have a pesky little attribute called...

position: absolute;

While I'm not going to say that in every case this is a bad thing, however in many I think it does lack a little je ne sais quoi if you will. Rather, it means you could have programmed in a much better and more efficient way, and that's IF you consider HTML and CSS to be programming in the first place.

I'm not so sure I do. They're just unglorified google docs writing.

However, there is a bright side to this story. I found a wonderful little site that if you're here, well, either you're my mom. Hi mom! Or you program at least a little (by that I mean you can google the hell out of anything) and you know a site called https://www.w3schools.com/. Life savers you are W3 Schools, truly.

It wasn't that I didn't know there was a background-image property in CSS, it's more that unless I've used something a lot I generally will believe I don't know how to use it then I'll go to the interwebs as a handy dandy lil free guide. Unfortunately that'll lead you astray sometime but alas I digress.

When I went to that wonderful little tutorial I got my hero background image squared away in no-time. Thus leaving plenty of time for the upgrades the title of this post so pulled you in about.

SIDE MENUUUUUU

That's right slime. Did I write it in my little todo list the other day. Well I don't remember, you could take a little looksie if you care so much but I've had the idea cooking for a while so it makes me so unreasonably happy that I got that bad boy working. The reason I included that whole preamble bad boy is precisely because my little position absolute oppsie was making it impossible to make the side menu even a fathomable next step.

Slime. I'm gonna keep calling you whoever is reading this slime. Like a little love letter but I don't know you and instead of a cute name its slime (but in my head I see a little green slime ball with googley eyes so it's not that I think poorly of you) just a cute little pile of goop, that's all.

Anyways, shlime. I hope you think the cute little side-menu bar is as cute as I do you googley-eyed little guy. Have such a wonderful day and I'll catch ya next time!

What if I just didn't give any Fs about making this look pretty whatsoever? Like sure somewhat here and there but really nothing besides a little css to put it all in the center and separating the paragraphs when I feel like it.

Like if we are going to be honest, and I will since I can... This blog is for me and only me so I get to just mess around with it. While it isn't meant to be a train wreck I do think there is more value to me as an individual and as someone who is constantly evolving and learning her own style of design to just DO and not think so much about it all the time.

So here's what I am going to do! (Whether you like it or not, I really couldn't care less, you.) I am just going to write them all on one page for now. RIGHT HERE!! Yep, so literally every single blog post will just go right above this one. How fun and charming!!

Here are some of the things that I would love to later on implement:

  1. Add a go back to top button (https://www.w3schools.com/howto/howto_js_scroll_to_top.asp)
  2. A "See More..." button that hides and unhides all but the most recent post on this page
  3. Separate blog post pages and one central "Heres the start of the blog!" page
  4. Make it so that those pages can be previewed (image or title etc.)
  5. Using flask and SQL make a function or mini web app so I can type each part into a form of sorts and just click a button and it makes the blog post for me on its own page

What can I say? A girl has got big dreams for a blog with a sky blue background and nothing else on it. But you know what I have always spent SOOOOOO much time worrying about making things perfect that I never actually just get to the doing part. And that sucks because the doing is the best part of any project, not the worrying or planning it.

For tonight since it is nearly 1am, I am going to call it but this is good and I'll keep making updates. I will also 100% maybe probably but I really should put this on github and figure out how to host it too.

'Til morrow
